## Part 1: Introduction

### Required sections

**1. Context and Problem Statement** (2-3 pages)
- Research background
- Problem identification
- Motivation for research

**2. Related Work** (3-4 pages)
- Literature review
- Existing solutions
- Research gap identification

**3. Objectives and Scope** (1-2 pages)
- Research objectives (numbered list)
- Scope definition (in-scope/out-of-scope)
- Expected outcomes

**4. Research Content** (2-3 pages)
- Research methodology
- Technical approach
- Development phases

**5. Thesis Outline** (1 page)
- Structure overview
- Chapter summaries

## Part 2: Thesis content

### Structure

Minimum 3 chapters.

### Typical organization

**Chapter 1: Background and Related Work** (10-15 pages)
- Theoretical foundations
- Technology overview
- Related research
- Comparative analysis

**Chapter 2: Design and Implementation** (15-25 pages)
- System architecture
- Database design
- Algorithm design
- Implementation details
- User interface design

**Chapter 3: Testing and Evaluation** (8-12 pages)
- Testing methodology
- Test cases and results
- Performance evaluation
- Usability testing
- Results analysis

## Part 3: Conclusion and future work

**I. Conclusion** (3-5 pages)
- Summary of achievements
- Research contributions
- Limitations
- Lessons learned

**II. Future Work** (2-3 pages)
- Potential improvements
- Future research directions
- Recommendations

## Section numbering rules

### Chapters within parts

Chapters are numbered globally (CHAPTER 1, CHAPTER 2, CHAPTER 3), not per-part.

### Sections within chapters

Sections use hierarchical numbering:

```
CHAPTER 1: BACKGROUND
  1.1 First section
    1.1.1 First subsection
      1.1.1.1 First sub-subsection
    1.1.2 Second subsection
  1.2 Second section

CHAPTER 2: DESIGN
  2.1 First section
    2.1.1 First subsection
  2.2 Second section
```

### Part headings

Part headings (PART 1, PART 2, PART 3) are visual separators. They are not numbered and do not appear in the table of contents hierarchy — only chapters and sections are numbered.

## Writing order

1. Start with Part 1 and complete all sections
2. Write chapters sequentially (1 → 2 → 3)
3. Write Part 3 last (reflect on completed work)
4. Write abstract after main content is final
5. Write acknowledgements last
